Immediate Safety

Ask if the children are with a safe person right now. If they are still at home or with the person using violence, we need to think about a safe way to get them out. Do they have a safe place to go tonight, like a relative's house or a shelter?

Practical Help

If they had to leave home quickly, do the children have what they need for tomorrow? Think about school uniforms, school bags, or basic clothes. Can the school be told about the situation so they can help keep the children safe while they are in class?

Rights & Support

Children are survivors too. Ask if there are any current Family Court orders or Apprehended Violence Orders (AVOs) that include the children. Later on, they might need specialist counselling to help them heal from the trauma of what they have seen or heard.

Remember: You aren't a counsellor, but asking these simple questions helps keep children safe.
